title: Build a Pitch Deck in React
description: A practical walkthrough for turning a startup pitch deck into a React-based presentation that stays on-brand and easy to iterate on.
---

Most pitch decks are built under pressure.

You need something polished, fast, and easy to update. The problem is that pitch decks also tend to become living assets. Numbers change. Messaging changes. Screenshots change. The product evolves.

That is why a code-first deck can be surprisingly useful.

With Slide-CN, you can build a pitch deck in React using the same mindset you already use for product surfaces: reusable layouts, consistent tokens, and components instead of repeated manual formatting.

# Start with a simple deck structure

A strong first version often looks like this:

1. Title slide
2. Problem
3. Solution
4. Product demo
5. Market or category
6. Traction
7. Why now
8. Team
9. Closing ask

In Slide-CN, that might look like:

```tsx
<Deck>
  <Slide><PitchIntroSlide /></Slide>
  <Slide><ProblemSlide /></Slide>
  <Slide><SolutionSlide /></Slide>
  <Slide><ProductDemoSlide /></Slide>
  <Slide><MarketSlide /></Slide>
  <Slide><TractionSlide /></Slide>
  <Slide><WhyNowSlide /></Slide>
  <Slide><TeamSlide /></Slide>
  <Slide><ClosingSlide /></Slide>
</Deck>
```

This is already a useful shift.

Instead of thinking in isolated pages, you are thinking in narrative components.

## Use reusable layouts, not custom slide designs every time

One of the easiest mistakes in a pitch deck is over-designing each slide independently.

That usually leads to inconsistency.

A better approach is to choose two or three layout patterns and repeat them:

- title slide
- split layout
- metric or proof slide
- closing slide

For example:

```tsx
<HorizontalSplit
  left={
    <>
      <h2 className="text-4xl font-semibold">The problem</h2>
      <p className="mt-4 text-xl text-muted-foreground">
        Teams still build presentations in tools disconnected from their product stack.
      </p>
    </>
  }
  right={<ProblemVisual />}
/>
```

That gives the deck rhythm without making it feel repetitive.

## Keep the deck on-brand with your existing tokens

If your team already has:

- typography choices
- brand colors
- spacing conventions
- UI motifs

you can bring those directly into the presentation.

The result is not just "nicer slides." It is a more credible story. The deck feels like it belongs to the product, not adjacent to it.

## Show the product as a product, not as screenshots on white rectangles

Pitch decks often fall into screenshot overload.

A better approach is to show the product in context:

- zoom in on one core workflow
- sequence UI states across multiple slides
- use progressive reveal for one important interaction
- highlight the outcome, not every detail

## When a React pitch deck is a great idea

Use this approach when:

- your product already has a strong visual identity
- your team is comfortable with React
- the deck will be updated frequently
- you want slides to feel tightly connected to the product

## When it is not

Skip this approach if:

- you need lots of non-technical collaborators to edit the deck directly
- the deck is a one-time artifact with minimal design stakes
- speed of drag-and-drop editing matters more than consistency
